01

Polymer waste upcycling

Polymers are ubiquitous in our society and produced at a large scale. Due to the low recycle rate, they create huge amount of non-degradable waste. We catalytically convert polymer waste into fuels and chemicals. We aim at improving the efficiency of the processes through mechanism-informed catalyst design, developing catalytic systems compatible with common additives and impurities, and understand how the structure and behavior of the polymers affect the reaction.

02

H2 Generation and Storage

H2 occupies the central role in the future vision for a sustainable and decarbonized economy. Currently, H2 generation heavily relies on fossil fuels, and it faces challenges in efficient transportation. We develop catalytic systems to generate green, renewable H2, and store them in liquid carriers reversibly. We aim at exploring new catalyst space for these reactions, understanding the behaviors of reactive species on the catalysts, and applying concepts from homogeneous catalysis in these heterogeneous catalytic processes.​

03

Precise Novel Catalyst Design

Most heterogeneous catalysts have non-uniform, ill-controlled active sites. This reduces their efficiency and hinders the understanding of catalysis science. We develop novel catalysts with rationally designed, well-controlled structures, for various applications. We aim at acquiring more precise understanding regarding structure-function relationship and reaction mechanisms, in addition to improving catalyst performances. We are also interested in advancing the toolbox for catalyst synthesis and spectroscopic characterization.​
